Abstract

If it were possible to seed a “baby universe,” any outgoing communication must be encoded at creation and persist cosmologically. We propose a τ-first, operational taxonomy of one-shot message channels that are universal, robust to inflation and late-time astrophysics, and decodable by any advanced observers. We list concrete, quantified “beacons” (uniform CMB polarization rotation, spectral features in the primordial gravitational-wave background, topology tags) with detection benchmarks and falsifiers, and outline a τ-budgeting approach to ensure signals remain benign for structure formation.

1. Motivation & Scope

The seeding entity and the offspring cosmos are generically causally disconnected. Thus, “contact” reduces to leaving global, dimensionless, and redundant signatures that any civilization could recover with sky surveys or precision cosmology.

2. Foundations: Causality & Baby Universes

Pinch-off geometries and black-hole interiors typically sever two-way links. We therefore focus on initial-condition encodings that propagate to late times with minimal degradation.

Design target: global, frame-independent, horizon-scale observables → universal accessibility

3. One-Shot Message Principles

  • Universality: everyone sees it (CMB, primordial GWs, topology).
  • Survivability: resistant to inflationary smoothing and late-time processing.
  • Decodability: compressible into simple ratios/angles; low algorithmic complexity.
  • Benign: does not spoil BBN, CMB, or structure formation.
  • Redundancy: the same number appears via two independent mechanisms.

4. Universal Channels

4.1 CMB polarization rotation (cosmic birefringence)

Encode a frequency-independent angle θ that mixes E/B modes.

Signature: nonzero TB/EB with uniform sign → rotation angle θ

4.2 Primordial gravitational-wave background (PGWB) beacons

Generate two–three narrow peaks with special ratios, robust across detectors.

Signature: ΩGW(f) has peaks at f1, f2, ... with f2/f1 = φ (golden ratio), etc.

4.3 Spatial topology tag

Choose a multiply connected fundamental domain with integer side-length ratios (e.g., 3:4:5) just inside the observable radius.

Signature: matched circles in the CMB + repeating LSS patterns

4.4 Parity-odd imprints

Chiral PGWB or scale-invariant TB/EB beyond uniform rotation.

4.5 Mild isocurvature markers

Small, coherent isocurvature fraction at two k-modes with a simple ratio.

5. Concrete Beacons & Benchmarks

Design note: choose dimensionless encodings (angles, ratios) and keep amplitudes below conservative cosmological bounds.

5.1 Uniform CMB rotation

Target: θ = 0.0314159 rad (≈ 1.80°) → recoverable from TB/EB

Redundancy: also encode θ/π in a second channel (e.g., chiral PGWB sign).

5.2 Two-peak PGWB “golden beacon”

f2/f1 = φ = (1 + √5)/2 ≈ 1.61803

Keep peak amplitudes modest; prioritize ratios over absolutes.

5.3 Topology ID

Fundamental domain: side ratios 3:4:5; curvature tuned to produce matched circles at degree scales

5.4 “Lockstep” checksum

δλC/λC = δtC/tC = −δωc/ωc

Include a tiny, coherent modulation obeying this relation so multiple experiments confirm the same bit.

6. τ-First Engineering Notes

Let τ ≡ E/c3 ≡ m/c. Treat message generation as τ-budgeted excitations:

Emsg → τmsg = Emsg/c3

Constraints: keep total τmsg small enough to avoid excess radiation or curvature while preserving detectability.

7. Detection Roadmap

  • CMB: TB/EB estimators for uniform rotation; topology matched-circle searches.
  • PGWB: cross-band spectral peak hunting (PTAs, space interferometers, ground-based).
  • LSS: wiggle-finding in P(k); topology repeats.
  • Redundancy: same number via two channels to suppress systematics.

8. Falsifiers & Failure Modes

  • Null TB/EB consistent with calibration → rules out uniform rotation beacon.
  • No stable PGWB peak ratios across bands → rules out spectral encoding.
  • Matched-circle limits push topology scale beyond horizon → topology tag excluded.
  • Any signal that breaks BBN/CMB/structure limits → not a viable beacon.

9. Ethical & Design Considerations

  • Non-maleficence: do not degrade structure formation or habitability.
  • Universality: avoid anthropic encodings; prefer mathematical constants and simple ratios.
  • Verification: design for multi-probe confirmation within a few decades of detectors.
